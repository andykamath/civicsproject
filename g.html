Governmental Structure

What makes our Government so strong?

There are a lot of factors that make the American Government so strong. Through concepts such as Federalism, Seperation of Powers, and judicial processes, our government breaks down work into smaller and smaller responsibilities so people have to work together to find a solution to a problem as opposed to one man doing everything. When one man does everything, as we have seen in the past, it is both very unreliable and that person may not be able to handle the stress level. Federalism breaks down powers into country and state issues so that the state can deal with problems specific to itself. Seperation of Powers helps keep each branch of government in check. Agencies help the President make decisions and speed up the legislative processes.

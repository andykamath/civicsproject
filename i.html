What led to America's political success?

After the American Revolution, there were a lot of contributing factors to our government that made it the power that it is today. The colonists first sought to do everythingin the exact opposite manner of that of Great Britain thus leading to the Articles of Confederation. This sucked however, and was a pathetic piece of literature. The Founding Fathers eventually got together to improve the AoC but instead just used the ideas from The Englightenment to write the Constitution. After the Constitution was written, many people still opposed it since it focused very little on people's actual rights. This is when we had the first ten amendments to the Constitution, thus creating the United States Bill of Rights.

These articles were heavily influenced by ideas and things from the Enlightenment. For exxample, the Magna Carta gave the idea of people's actual rights written down. The Social Contract added ideas of relationship between Government and people. Montesquieu and Locke also gave many ideas on the relationship between Government and the people and the separation of Church and State.
